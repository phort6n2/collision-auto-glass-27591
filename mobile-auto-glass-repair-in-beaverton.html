What is ADAS calibration and why do I need it after windshield replacement?
ADAS (Advanced Driver Assistance Systems) calibration is required when your windshield has cameras or sensors for features like lane departure warning, automatic emergency braking, or adaptive cruise control. When we replace your windshield, these systems need to be recalibrated to ensure they're properly aligned and functioning safely. We use manufacturer-approved calibration equipment and follow OEM specifications. This process typically adds 1-2 hours to your service but is essential for your safety systems to work correctly. Oregon doesn't legally require it, but we strongly recommend it for any vehicle with these features.
How long does the mobile service take, and when can I drive my car again?
Most windshield replacements take 60-90 minutes, plus additional time for ADAS calibration if needed. We typically schedule 2-3 hour appointments to ensure we're not rushed. You can drive your vehicle immediately after installation, but we recommend waiting at least 1 hour before driving to allow the adhesive to set properly. We'll provide you with specific drive-away instructions and tape guidelines to follow. For side window repairs, you can usually drive right away. We'll work around your schedule and can often accommodate same-day or next-day appointments in the Beaverton area.
